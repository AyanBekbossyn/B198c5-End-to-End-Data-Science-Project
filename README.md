# B198c5-End-to-End-Data-Science-Project

Today, the railway transport system is an integral part of daily life for city dwellers. For a country like the United Kingdom, where the public transport system carries millions of passengers, it is crucial to understand and predict future delays in order to mitigate risks and ideally, eliminate the factors that contribute to train delays.

The goal of this project was to determine whether transport delays could be predicted using historical operational data alongside influencing factors such as weather conditions and public holidays. The project utilized official railway performance statistics published by the Office of Rail and Road (ORR), combined with historical weather data from Open-Meteo and information on UK public holidays. After clustering the datasets, several machine learning models were employed to predict the total minutes of delay for railway trains.
